'''Twardogóra''' {{IPAr|pl|t|f|a|r|d|o|'|g|u|r|a}} (formerly German ''Festenberg'') is a town in [[Oleśnica County]], [[Lower Silesian Voivodeship]], in south-western [[Poland]]. It is the seat of the administrative district ([[gmina]]) called [[Gmina Twardogóra]]. Prior to 1945 it was in [[Germany]]. On 23 January 1945 the town was taken by the [[Red Army]] and later the same year became a part of [[Poland]]. (For more information on the history of the region, see [[Silesia]].)

It lies approximately {{convert|20|km|mi|0}} north of [[Oleśnica]], and {{convert|40|km|mi|0}} north-east of the regional capital [[Wrocław]].

As at 2006, the town has a population of 6,866.
