The [[constellation]] '''Circinus''' is a small [[constellation]] in the southern sky, first defined in the 18th century. Its name is [[Latin]] for [[compass (drafting)|compass]], referring to the [[drafting]] tool used for drawing circles; it should not be confused with [[Pyxis]], a constellation that represents a [[mariner's compass]].

==History==

Circinus was introduced in the 18th century by [[Nicolas Louis de Lacaille]], whose fascination with secular science led him to name the constellations he defined after scientific instruments. 

==Location==

Circinus is located at a [[declination]] of −50 to −60 degrees, and hence the whole constellation is visible only south of the [[30th parallel north|30° north]] [[latitude]].
