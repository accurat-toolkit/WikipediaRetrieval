'''Lake Carey''', ({{coord|29|5|4|S|122|18|56|E|type:waterbody_region:AU}}), is a [[salt lake]] {{convert|80|km|mi|0}} south of [[Laverton, Western Australia|Laverton]], [[Western Australia]] within the Laverton Tectonic Zone, that has been associated with the presence of [[gold]] since the 1890s {{ref|Linden}} and recently there has been renewed exploration drilling. Midas, the company who have sponsored the drilling, estimates that there may be 25 million ounces (780 tonnes) of gold {{ref|gold}} within the site. It has also been reported that there may well be significant amounts of nickel and zinc. 

The [[Wangkathaa]] [[Indigenous Australians|people]] are associated with the land around Lake Carey{{ref|people}}.
