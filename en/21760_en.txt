==Events== 
*[[1444]] &ndash; [[Battle of Varna]]: The crusading forces of King [[Władysław III of Poland|Vladislaus III of Varna]] (aka ''Ulaszlo I of Hungary'' and ''Wladyslaw III of Poland'') are crushed by the [[Ottoman Empire|Turks]] under Sultan [[Murad II]] and Vladislaus is killed.
*[[1520]] &ndash; Danish [[Christian II of Denmark|King Christian II]] executes dozens of people in the [[Stockholm Bloodbath]] after a successful invasion of [[Sweden]].
*[[1619]] &ndash; [[René Descartes]] has the dreams that inspire his ''[[Meditations on First Philosophy]]''.
*[[1674]] &ndash; [[Anglo-Dutch War]]: As provided in the [[Treaty of Westminster (1674)|Treaty of Westminster]], [[Netherlands]] cedes [[New Netherlands]] to [[England]].  
*[[1766]] &ndash; The last colonial governor of [[New Jersey]], [[William Franklin]], signs the charter of Queen's College (later renamed [[Rutgers University]]).
*[[1775]] &ndash; The [[United States Marine Corps]] is [[United States Marine Corps birthday ball|founded]] at [[Tun Tavern]] in [[Philidelphia]] by [[Samuel Nicholas]].
*[[1793]] &ndash; A [[Goddess of Reason]] is proclaimed by the [[French Convention]] at the suggestion of [[Chaumette]].
*[[1847]] &ndash; The passenger ship ''[[Stephen Whitney (ship)|Stephen Whitney]]'' is wrecked in thick fog off the southern coast of Ireland, killing 92 of the 110 on board. The disaster results in the construction the [[Fastnet Rock|Fastnet Rock lighthouse]].
*[[1865]] &ndash; Major [[Henry Wirz]], the superintendent of [[Andersonville, Georgia#History|a prison camp in Andersonville, Georgia]], is [[hanging|hanged]], becoming the only [[American Civil War]] soldier [[execution (legal)|executed]] for [[war crime]]s.
*[[1871]] &ndash; [[Henry Morton Stanley]] locates missing explorer and missionary, Dr. [[David Livingstone]] in [[Ujiji]], near  [[Lake Tanganyika]], allegedly greeting him with the words,  "Dr. Livingstone, I presume?"
*[[1910]] &ndash; The date of [[Thomas A. Davis]]' opening of the [[San Diego Army and Navy Academy]], though the official founding date is [[November 23]], 1910.
*[[1918]] &ndash; The Western Union Cable Office in [[North Sydney, Nova Scotia]] receives a top-secret coded message from Europe (that would be sent to [[Ottawa, Ontario]] and [[Washington, DC]]) that said on [[November 11, 1918]] all fighting would cease on land, sea and in the air. 
*[[1919]] &ndash; The first national convention of the [[American Legion]] is held in [[Minneapolis, Minnesota]], ending on [[November 12]].   
*[[1924]] &ndash; [[Dion O'Banion]], leader of the [[North Side Gang]] is assassinated in his flower shop by members of [[Johnny Torrio]]'s gang, sparking the bloody gang war of the 1920s in Chicago.
*[[1928]] &ndash; [[Hirohito|Michinomiya Hirohito]] is crowned the 124th [[Emperor of Japan]], Emperor Showa. 
*[[1938]] &ndash; [[Mustafa Kemal Atatürk]], the founder of the [[Republic of Turkey]], dies.
*[[1940]] &ndash; [[Walt Disney]] begins serving as an informer for the [[Los Angeles]] office of the [[FBI]]; his job is to report back information on [[Hollywood]] subversives.
*[[1942]] &ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Nazi Germany|Germany]] [[Case Anton|invades Vichy France]] following French Admiral [[François Darlan]]'s agreement to an armistice with the [[Allies of World War II|Allies]] in [[North Africa]].
*[[1944]] &ndash; The ammunition ship [[USS Mount Hood (AE-11)|USS ''Mount Hood'' (AE-11)]] explodes at Seeadler Harbour, [[Manus Island|Manus]], [[Admiralty Islands]]  
*[[1945]] &ndash; [[Battle of Surabaya|Heavy fighting]] in [[Surabaya]] between [[Indonesia]]n nationalists and returning colonialists after World War II, is celebrated as Heroes' Day (Hari Pahlawan).
*[[1951]] &ndash; Direct-dial coast-to-coast [[telephone]] service begins in the [[United States]]. 
*[[1954]] &ndash; U.S. President [[Dwight D. Eisenhower]] dedicates the [[USMC War Memorial]] (Iwo Jima memorial) in [[Arlington National Cemetery]].  
*[[1958]] &ndash; The [[Hope Diamond]] is donated to the [[Smithsonian Institution]] by [[New York]] diamond merchant [[Harry Winston]].
*[[1969]] &ndash; [[National Educational Television]] (the predecessor to the [[Public Broadcasting Service]]) in the [[United States]] debuts the children's [[television]] program ''[[Sesame Street]]''.
*[[1970]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: [[Vietnamization]] &ndash; For the first time in five years, an entire week ends with no reports of [[United States|American]] combat fatalities in [[Southeast Asia]].  
*  1970   &ndash; The Soviet Lunar probe [[Lunokhod 1]] is launched.
*[[1971]] &ndash; In [[Cambodia]], [[Khmer Rouge]] forces attack the city of [[Phnom Penh]] and its airport, killing 44, wounding at least 30 and damaging nine [[fixed-wing aircraft|aircraft]].   
*[[1972]] &ndash; [[Southern Airways]] Flight 49 from [[Birmingham, Alabama]] is [[Aircraft hijacking|hijacked]] and, at one point, is threatened with crashing into the nuclear installation at the [[Oak Ridge National Laboratory]]. After two days, the plane lands in [[Havana]], [[Cuba]], where the hijackers are jailed by [[Fidel Castro]].
*[[1975]] &ndash; The 729-foot-long freighter [[SS Edmund Fitzgerald|SS ''Edmund Fitzgerald'']] sinks during a storm on [[Lake Superior]], killing all 29 crew on board.  
*  1975   &ndash; [[United Nations General Assembly Resolution 3379|United Nations Resolution 3379]]: [[United Nations]] General Assembly approves a resolution equating [[Zionism]] with [[racism]] (the resolution is repealed in December 1991 with [[UN General Assembly Resolution 4686|Resolution 4686]]).  
*[[1989]] &ndash; Fall of the communist regime in [[Bulgaria]].
*[[1995]] &ndash; In [[Nigeria]], playwright and environmental activist [[Ken Saro-Wiwa]], along with eight others from the [[Movement for the Survival of the Ogoni People]] (Mosop), are hanged by government forces.   
*[[1997]] &ndash; [[WorldCom]] and [[MCI Communications]] announce a $37 billion merger (the largest merger in US history at the time).   
*[[2006]] &ndash; [[Sri Lankan Tamil]] Parliamentarian [[Nadarajah Raviraj]] is assassinated in [[Colombo]].
*[[2007]] &ndash; ''[[¿Por qué no te callas?]]'' incident between [[Juan Carlos I of Spain|King Juan Carlos]] of [[Spain]] and [[Venezuela|Venezuela's president]] [[Hugo Chávez]].
